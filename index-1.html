✦ Moon Phase Complication ✦
How to read & set the moon phase
on a mechanical wristwatch
01
Locate the complication
Find the moon phase display on your dial — typically a small aperture at 6 o'clock. Inside sits a rotating disc with two painted moons visible through a semi-circular window representing the sky.
02
Note the last full moon date
Check the Last Full Moon date shown on this app's main screen, and count how many days ago it occurred. This is the number of advances you will need to make in step 04.
03
Set the disc to full moon
Unscrew the crown if needed, then pull it to the setting position (two clicks out on most watches). Advance the moon disc — via the crown or a dedicated pusher at 2 or 4 o'clock depending on your calibre — until the moon is perfectly centred in the aperture, fully visible. This represents the moment of full moon.
04
Advance one press per day elapsed
From the full moon position, press or advance the complication once for each day that has passed since the last full moon. For example, if the last full moon was 8 days ago, advance the disc 8 times. Each press moves the disc forward by exactly one day.
05
Push the crown back in
Once set, push the crown back to its normal position and screw it down if applicable. The disc will now advance automatically — roughly one day every 24 hours — keeping pace with the lunar cycle.
Accuracy: Most mechanical moon phase complications use a 59-tooth wheel that advances every 24 hours, which drifts by about one day every 2.7 years compared to the true 29.53-day synodic month. High-precision versions use a 135-tooth wheel, accurate to roughly one day per 122 years. Periodically resetting using this app keeps yours true.
